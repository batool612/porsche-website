About Porsche

History

Porsche is a German automobile manufacturer that specializes in high-performance sports cars, SUVs, and sedans. It was founded in 1931 by Ferdinand Porsche, an Austrian automotive engineer. The company initially offered consulting services and vehicle development work, but it soon started producing its own cars.

One of the most iconic Porsche models is the 911, which was first introduced in 1963 and has since become synonymous with the brand. Over the years, Porsche has continued to innovate and push the boundaries of automotive engineering.

Mission

Porsche's mission is to create exceptional sports cars that deliver exhilarating driving experiences while also prioritizing quality, performance, and innovation. The company strives to combine cutting-edge technology with timeless design, resulting in vehicles that are both thrilling to drive and a joy to own.

Notable Achievements

  • 24 Hours of Le Mans: Porsche has a rich history of success at the 24 Hours of Le Mans, one of the most prestigious endurance races in the world. The company has won the race overall a record 19 times, including numerous victories in the 1970s and 1980s.
  • Formula One: Although Porsche has not competed in Formula One as a constructor since the 1960s, the company has achieved success as an engine supplier. Porsche-powered cars have won multiple Formula One races and championships.
  • World Endurance Championship (WEC): Porsche has enjoyed recent success in the WEC, winning the overall championship and the 24 Hours of Le Mans in both 2015 and 2016 with its Porsche 919 Hybrid prototype.
